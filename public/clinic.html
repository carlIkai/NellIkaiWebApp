Mellem Clinic

The word “mellem” means “between.” What happens in the space between two people can be perplexing as we bring perception, bias, hopes, fears, longing and more into every interaction we have. Therapy helps us understand what happens in that space between two individuals. Where to begin with this work? In the privacy and safety of the therapeutic space.

Mellem Clinic is a collaboration of dedicated practitioners and trainees devoted to the practice and integration of psychodynamic psychotherapy. As the landscape of mental healthcare evolves, we impress the importance of a foundation of psychoanalytic thought as cornerstones of evolving practice. Providing accessibility and equity in psychotherapy services is our mission.

As a training clinic, we are dedicated to nurturing and developing the therapy skills of our clinicians-in-training. Our approach to therapy is guided by the principles of psychoanalytic theory. Our goal is to help our patients achieve a deeper understanding of themselves, resolve long-standing issues, and develop more satisfying relationships.

Providers

Dr. Inga Korsgaard in the founder and clinical director of Mellem Clinic llc. She completed her Master’s and Doctorate in Clinical Psychology at The Derner School of Psychology (Garden City, New York). She completed her clinical training at various locations in Brooklyn and Manhattan including Maimonides Medical Center, Lenox Hill Hospital, Coney Island Hospital and also has comprehensive experience in private practice, community mental health clinics, and college counseling centers. Dr. Korsgaard is a member of The American Psychological Association (APA), Society for Psychoanalysis and Psychoanalytic Psychology (Division 39, APA), and the Rhode Island Association for Psychodynamic Psychologies (RIAPP). She currently mentors psychotherapy trainees in New York, Massachusetts, and Rhode Island.

Nell is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker offering therapy to adults and couples in Rhode Island. Prior to joining the Mellem Clinic, Nell worked and trained at Prevention Point Philadelphia, Butler Hospital, Stanley Street Treatment and Resources, and the Providence VA Medical Center. Nell provides psychotherapy to clients struggling with a variety of mental health concerns, including grief, loss, depression, interpersonal challenges, anxiety, substance use, post-traumatic stress, and chronic illness. Nell believes that therapy is a collaborative process and approaches her work with warmth and curiosity. She uses a psychodynamic and relational approach to help clients gain a sense of safety, awareness, and a compassionate understanding of themselves and the world around them. Nell is a graduate of the Boston University School of Social Work and a member of the National Association of Social Workers, Rhode Island Chapter.

Jack is a graduate of the Smith College School for Social Work and a member of the National Association of Social Workers, Massachusetts Chapter. Jack provides psychotherapy to adolescents, college students, and adults of all ages in Rhode Island and Massachusetts. Jack is clinically trained in psychodynamic, relational, and attachment theories. Jack brings a therapeutic approach that is nurturing, collaborative, and informed by an anti-oppressive framework. Jack seeks to offer an affirming space where clients can receive support metabolizing trauma and restore a felt sense of safety, trust, and re-connection to one’s self. Jack also provides therapy to clients navigating challenges of grief, loss, dissociation, depression, body dysphoria, anxiety, experiences of bullying, valuing oneself and one’s intersectional identity, transition, self-compassion, and valued relationships. Jack is passionate about shining a light on clients’ strengths and learning about what each individual finds meaningful in their life.
